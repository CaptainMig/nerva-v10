What is NERVA?

NERVA is a decision integrity engine. It takes a situation — any high-stakes decision — and evaluates whether the conditions are right to act, wait, escalate to a human, or veto entirely.

It does this by mapping the decision into a mathematical state space borrowed from quantum information theory. The result is a single, auditable signal: COMMIT, HOLD, WAIT, ESCALATE, or TOXIC.

⚠ One-Way Brake

Some decisions can't be undone. When NERVA detects a low-reversibility situation with an incomplete foundation, it automatically escalates — even if all other signals say go. This is the One-Way Brake: a hard override that routes irreversible decisions to human review, no matter what.

# NERVA v8.3.2

Decision Integrity Layer for Autonomous Systems

Built by Phasewell Research. Mathematically grounded in quantum information theory.

What It Does

Autonomous systems — drones, trading algorithms, surgical robots, self-driving vehicles — make decisions at machine speed. Most guardrails either slow things down too much or can't explain themselves after the fact.

NERVA sits between a proposed action and its execution. It evaluates the decision state and returns one of five verdicts — with full mathematical provenance for every call.

Proposed action → 6D state vector → Bloch sphere projection → COMMIT | HOLD | WAIT | ESCALATE | TOXIC

How It Works

Each decision is mapped to six input dimensions: urgency, strategy quality, risk exposure, evidence support, environmental stability. These collapse into a Bloch vector — a point inside a unit sphere representing the decision state.

From that state, NERVA computes density matrix metrics — entropy, purity, coherence — and derives an information-theoretic commitment threshold. The threshold adapts to the decision: higher uncertainty and higher stakes raise the bar for action.

ρ = ½(I + r_x·σ_x + r_y·σ_y + r_z·σ_z) Entropy: S(ρ) = -λ₊ log₂(λ₊) - λ₋ log₂(λ₋) Purity: Tr(ρ²) = ½(1 + |r|²) ∈ [0.5, 1] Threshold: τ = 0.25 + 0.35·S(ρ) + 0.25·R − 0.20·reversibility

Safety Overrides

One-Way Brake: If a decision is irreversible (low reversibility score) and the integrity foundation is incomplete, NERVA forces ESCALATE regardless of other signals. Irreversible decisions require a higher standard of evidence — this is enforced mathematically, not by policy.

EV Block: If expected value is negative, COMMIT is suppressed. The system won't recommend action when the math says the outcome is likely harmful.

TOXIC veto: Low integrity combined with high urgency and pressure triggers a hard veto. No autonomous action. Human authorization required.

Mathematical Provenance

NERVA's formalism is grounded in Nielsen & Chuang (2010), Shannon (1948), and Baumgratz et al. (2014). The v8.3.2 normalization correction ensures internal consistency across all metrics — the density matrix, entropy, purity, and coherence all derive from the same normalized Bloch vector by construction.
